Kentaro Miura

Berserk's Author

Kentaro Miura, who reached a worldwide readership for more than 30 years with his Japanese manga series “Berserk”

Here's a time line of Kentaro Miura's life:

  • 1966 - Miura was born in Chiba, Chiba Prefecture, Japan
  • 1976 - At the age of 10, he created his first manga, entitled Miuranger, which was published for his classmates in a school publication; the series ended up spanning 40 volumes.
  • 1977 - Miura created his second manga, Ken e no Michi "The Way to the Sword", in which he used India ink for the first time.
  • 1979 - When he was in middle school his drawing techniques improved greatly as he started using professional drawing techniques.
  • 1982 - Miura enrolled in an artistic curriculum, where he and his classmates started publishing their works in school booklets. There, he befriended his later fellow manga artist Kōji Mori
  • 1984 - Miura briefly worked as an assistant to George Morikawa, of Hajime no Ippo fame. Morikawa quickly acknowledged Miura's high artistic level and dismissed him, saying there was nothing he could teach that Miura did not already know.
  • 1985 - Miura applied for entrance to the art college of Nihon University. He submitted a short project, Futatabi "Once More", for examination and was granted admission. The project later earned him the 34th Newcomer Manga Award from Weekly Shōnen Magazine.
  • 1988 - Miura published a prototype of Berserk in Hakusensha's Monthly ComiComi; the 48-page prototype placed second in ComiComi's 7th Manga School competition.
  • 1989 - The full serialization of Berserk, which would become Miura's most famous and successful work, began in Hakusensha's Monthly Animal House.
  • 1992 - Monthly Animal House was renamed Young Animal, where Berserk continued serialization. In the same year.
  • 2002 - Miura received the Award for Excellence at the 6th Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prizes for Berserk.
  • 2006 - Berserk went on frequent and often extended hiatuses, and alternated between monthly and irregular serialization.
  • 2013 - Miura released the short standalone manga Giganto Maxia.
  • 2019 - Duranki, a short manga produced by Miura's personal manga studio Studio Gaga, was serialized in Young Animal Zero.
  • 2021 - On May 6, Miura died due to an acute aortic dissection, at the age of 54. His death was announced on May 20. A private ceremony was held by his family.
